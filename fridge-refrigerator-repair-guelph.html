Refrigerator Repair Costs & Common Issues

Refrigerators are the most expensive appliance to lose — not just the repair cost, but the food loss if it fails overnight. A service call runs $80–$120, applied toward the repair. Start relay replacement, one of the most common fridge repairs, runs $80–$150. Defrost heater or thermostat issues, which cause frost buildup in the freezer and a warm fridge compartment, run $150–$280. Condenser fan motor replacement runs $130–$220, and a refrigerant leak diagnosis and recharge runs $250–$450 or more depending on the leak location — this is the one repair where cost can approach replacement value on older units.

Dirty condenser coils are worth checking before calling for service — vacuuming the coils behind or underneath the fridge is a genuine $0 fix that resolves a surprising number of "not cooling enough" complaints. Beyond that, the most common real fault is a failed start relay, which prevents the compressor from starting at all.

Refrigerators typically last 10–15 years. If the repair cost is under 50% of a comparable new fridge (typically $900–$2,500 installed) and your unit is under 6–7 years old, repair is usually the better financial call — with compressor failure on an older unit being the main exception worth weighing against replacement.
