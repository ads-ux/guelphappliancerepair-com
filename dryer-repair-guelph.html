Dryer Repair Costs & Common Issues

A service call in Guelph runs $80–$120, applied toward the repair. Heating element replacement, the single most common dryer repair, typically runs around $150. Thermal fuse replacement runs $100–$180 and is often triggered by restricted airflow rather than a random failure. Drum belt replacement — the fix when the drum won't turn even though the motor runs — costs $150–$250, and timer or control board repair runs $180–$320.

If your dryer is taking noticeably longer to dry a load than it used to, check the exhaust vent before assuming a mechanical fault — lint buildup restricting airflow through the vent duct is one of the most common causes of long cycles, and it's also a genuine house fire risk. We check vent airflow on every service call in addition to the heating element and thermostat.

Dryers typically last 10–13 years. If the repair is under 50% of a comparable new dryer's cost (typically $600–$1,200 installed) and your unit is under 5–6 years old, repair is usually the better financial call — and heating element or belt repairs are worth doing at almost any age given how inexpensive they are relative to replacement.
