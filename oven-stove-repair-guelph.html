Oven & Stove Repair Costs & Common Issues

A service call in Guelph runs $80–$120, which is applied toward the repair if you proceed. Heating element replacement — the most common electric oven repair — runs $150–$250. Igniter replacement on gas stoves and ovens runs $120–$200. Thermostat or temperature sensor issues, which cause inaccurate heating even when elements look fine, run $130–$220. Control board repair for dead displays or unresponsive buttons runs $200–$400 depending on the model.

On electric ranges, a visibly broken or bulging bake or broil element is the classic sign of failure and a straightforward fix. On gas ranges, a burner that clicks repeatedly without lighting usually points to a clogged igniter port (often fixable with cleaning) or a failed igniter module. Self-clean cycle failures are almost always either a faulty door lock mechanism — the cycle is designed not to run unless the safety lock engages — or a control board fault, both of which we repair without needing to replace the unit.

Ovens and stoves typically last 13–15 years. As a rule of thumb, if the repair cost is under 50% of a comparable new range (which runs $600–$1,200 installed in Guelph) and your unit is under 7–8 years old, repair is usually the better financial call.
